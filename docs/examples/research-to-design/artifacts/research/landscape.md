# Intake Landscape

## Purpose

Capture the current intake process, recurring friction points, and meaningful
constraints before proposing a revised workflow.

## Current Intake Channels

- website contact form
- direct email to the service team
- phone calls routed through front-desk staff

## Findings

### Finding 1

Current intake arrives through multiple channels that do not share the same
required information.

Basis:

- direct observation of the intake descriptions used in this example
- inference from the differing channel structure

Confidence: probable

### Finding 2

Front-desk staff often receive urgent requests before enough information exists
to assign the request cleanly.

Basis:

- example scenario assumption documented for this teaching artifact

Confidence: probable

### Finding 3

The current process mixes urgent triage, routine intake collection, and later
service assignment into one loose intake step.

Basis:

- inference from the current channel descriptions and pain points

Confidence: probable

## Recurring Friction Points

- repeated follow-up is needed to collect missing details
- urgent cases are hard to distinguish early
- staff use different language for the same intake state
- assignment decisions are delayed because the intake packet is uneven

## Constraints

- the service team needs enough information to route work responsibly
- front-desk staff need a quick path for urgent cases
- later design work should not assume one channel will replace all others

## Open Questions

- Which details are truly required before triage can happen?
- Which details can wait until after an initial response?
- Should phone intake follow the same path as form and email intake?
